Colors

The color theme for One-Click Contractor focuses on the details and uses color to express emotion, action, and prominence.

The color palette is comprised of nine core colors. The greys are used for backgrounds, borders and elements that are disabled. The brightest colors in the palette - red, yellow and green - are used to draw attention to a user. They maybe used for alerts, actions that cannot be undone, or descriptive labels that need extra attention. Finally, the most dominate colors - blue and green - are used for navigation and basic interaction tasks.

When adding new components or features, consider the usage first and then assign colors. By standardizing the palette and reserving certain colors for particular actions, the overall experience will benefit.

Typography

One-Click Contractor leverages one typeface and two weights for its interface. Roboto, designed by Google, gives the interface a professional look with just enough personality.

The dominate weight used is regular. This style of the font should be used in almost all cases. When a text element needs additional attention the bold weight can then be used. You will notice that the bold weight is used primarly for actions, labels, and areas where strong emphasis is needed. Below you will find various versions of the font and how it is to be used throughout the application's interface.
